2017-英语一-Section1

正文

  Could a hug a day keep doctor away?The answer may be a resounding *”yes!”*.
  Besides helping you feel close and connected to people you care about,it turns out that hugs can bring a host of health benefits for your body and mind.
  Believe it or not,a warm embrace might even help you avoid getting sick this winter.

  In the recent study involving 400 healthy adults,researchers from Carnegie Mellon University in Pennsylvania examined the effects of perceived social support and the receipt of hug on the participants’ susceptibility to developing common cold after beiong exposed to the virus.
  People who perceived social support were less likely to come down with a cold,and the researchers calculate that the stress-reducing effects of hugging explained about 32 percent of that benefitcial effect.
  Even among those who got a cold,the ones who felt greater social support and received more frequent hugs had less severe symptoms.

  ”Hugging protects people who are under stress from increased risk for colds that usually associated with stress,” notes Sheldon Cohen,a professor of psychology at Carnegie.
  Hugging “is a marker of intimacy and helps generate the feeling that others are there to help in the face of difficulty.”

  Some experts attribute the stress-reducing, health-related benefits of hugging to the release of oxytocin, often called “the bonding hormone” because it promotes attachment in relationships, includeing that between mothers and their newborn babies.
 Oxytocin is made primarily in the central lower part of the brain, and some of it is released into the bloodsteam.
  But some of it remains in the brain, where it influences mood,behavior and physiology.
